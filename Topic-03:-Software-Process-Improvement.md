### Goal
To introduce the concepts, models, and standards used to systematically improve the software development process. This topic focuses on understanding CMM, CMMI, and ISO 15504 (SPICE) as frameworks that guide organisations toward higher process maturity and quality.


### 🎯 Learning Objectives
Students should be able to:
1. Explain the concept of Software Process Improvement (SPI) and its importance.
2. Describe the Capability Maturity Model (CMM) and its staged approach.
3. Discuss the Capability Maturity Model Integration (CMMI) and its integrated structure.
4. Explain the Assessment Process Standard (ISO/IEC 15504 or SPICE) and how it measures process capability.
5. Compare the similarities and differences between CMM, CMMI, and ISO 15504 models.


### 📌 Topics to Cover                               
#### 1. Staged Models — Capability Maturity Model (CMM)
   - Developed by the Software Engineering Institute (SEI) at Carnegie Mellon University in the late 1980s.
   - Focuses on process maturity — how well software processes are defined, managed, measured, and optimised.
   - Divides organisational maturity into five levels:

| **Maturity Level**      | **Focus**                        | **Description**                                                                |
| ------------------------ | -------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Level 1 – Initial**    | Ad hoc                           | Unstructured process; success depends on individual effort.                    |
| **Level 2 – Repeatable** | Basic project management         | Policies and procedures established; processes repeatable for similar projects. |
| **Level 3 – Defined**    | Organization-wide standardization | Processes are documented and standardized across the organization.             |
| **Level 4 – Managed**    | Quantitative management          | Processes are measured and controlled using metrics.                           |
| **Level 5 – Optimizing** | Continuous improvement           | Organization focuses on innovation and proactive improvement.                  |

>Example: A company moves from Level 2 to Level 3 by standardising its coding and testing practices across all teams.

#### 2. Integrated Models — Capability Maturity Model Integration (CMMI)
   - Introduced by SEI to integrate various CMMs into a single model.
   - Provides a comprehensive framework for improving processes across disciplines such as software engineering, systems engineering, and project management.
   - Two main representations:
     * Staged Representation: Similar to the original CMM with five maturity levels.
     * Continuous Representation: Focuses on capability levels (0–5) for each process area independently.
   - Key Process Areas (KPAs):
     * Project Management (e.g., risk, configuration)
     * Engineering (e.g., requirements, design, testing)
     * Support (e.g., measurement, quality assurance)
     * Process Management (e.g., training, process definition)
>Purpose: Helps organisations assess process maturity and plan systematic improvements.
- Example: A software company adopts CMMI Level 3 to ensure consistent process documentation and defined workflows across projects.

#### 3. Assessment Process Standard — ISO/IEC 15504 (SPICE)
   - SPICE stands for Software Process Improvement and Capability Determination.
   - International standard for assessing software process capability and guiding process improvement.
   - Provides a framework for evaluating processes using capability levels (0–5):

| **Capability Level**      | **Process Attribute**         | **Description**                                 |
| -------------------------- | ----------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------- |
| **Level 0 – Incomplete**   | Not implemented               | Process not performed or fails to meet purpose. |
| **Level 1 – Performed**    | Performance management        | Process achieves its intended purpose.          |
| **Level 2 – Managed**      | Resource & product management | Process is planned, monitored, and controlled.  |
| **Level 3 – Established**  | Process definition            | Standard process defined and deployed.          |
| **Level 4 – Predictable**  | Measurement & control         | Process operates within defined limits.         |
| **Level 5 – Optimizing**   | Continuous improvement        | Focuses on process innovation and improvement.  |

> Purpose: ISO 15504 helps organisations understand how well processes are performed and what improvements are needed.

#### 💡 Relation to CMMI: ISO 15504 and CMMI share the same goal — improving process maturity — but ISO 15504 is an internationally recognized standard, while CMMI is a model framework.

### 💪 Hands-on Activity
1. CMM Maturity Simulation: Students evaluate a fictional company’s process maturity level based on a case study.
1. CMMI Mapping Exercise: Match each process area in CMMI to its maturity level.
1. ISO 15504 Self-Assessment: Teams use a checklist to determine the capability level of a given process (e.g., software testing).
