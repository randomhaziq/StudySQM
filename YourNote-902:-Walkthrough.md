## Introduction
A walkthrough is a peer-review technique in software quality assurance where the author of a document or code leads team members through the work product to gather feedback, identify defects, and improve quality.
>Walkthrough = Author-led review to find defects early and improve understanding.

## Purpose of a Walkthrough
Key reasons for conducting a walkthrough:
- Detect defects early (before coding or release)
- Improve the quality of documents or code
- Share knowledge among team members
- Verify that the product meets requirements
- Get feedback from different stakeholders
- Increase team understanding of the work product

## When Walkthroughs Are Used
Walkthroughs typically occur at:
- Requirements review
- Design review
- Code review
- Test case review
- User manual / documentation review
>Used early to avoid costly rework.

## Characteristics of a Walkthrough
1. Led by the author
   - The writer explains and demonstrates the work.

2. Informal or semi-formal
   - Less strict than an inspection.
   - No specific roles required.

3. Interactive session
   - Participants ask questions, suggest improvements, and discuss alternatives.

4. Focuses on understanding + defects
   - Not only bug finding—helps the team understand logic and requirements.

5. Flexible structure
   - Agenda varies depending on product or team needs.


## Roles Involved in a Walkthrough
Walkthroughs do not require strict roles, but commonly include:
- Author (Leader)
- Reviewers / Peers
- Scribe (Note Taker) (optional)
- Stakeholders/domain experts (optional)
>Unlike inspections, there is no moderator by default.

## Activities in a Walkthrough Session
Typical steps:
1. Author presents document/code.
2. Reviewers ask questions.
3. Team discusses logic, requirements, alternatives.
4. Potential defects are identified.
5. Author collects feedback for improvement.

## Benefits of Walkthroughs

- Improves clarity and correctness
- Reduces defects early
- Increases shared understanding
- Supports junior developers' learning
- Encourages team communication
- Improves maintainability (better code/design)

## Walkthrough vs Inspection vs Review
| Aspect             | **Walkthrough**            | **Inspection**                     | **Peer Review** |
| ------------------ | -------------------------- | ---------------------------------- | --------------- |
| **Leader**         | Author                     | Moderator                          | Peer            |
| **Formality**      | Semi-formal                | Formal                             | Informal        |
| **Goal**           | Understanding + defects    | Identify defects                   | Feedback        |
| **Roles Required** | Few                        | Many (Reader, Recorder, Moderator) | Minimal         |
| **Work Products**  | Requirements, design, code | All work products                  | All             |
