## 1. What is CMMI?

**CMMI (Capability Maturity Model Integration)** is a framework used to improve an organization's processes, quality, productivity, and project management practices. CMMI was developed by the Software Engineering Institute (SEI) at Carnegie Mellon University.

---

## 2. Why is CMMI Important?

Organizations use CMMI to:

- Improve software quality
- Deliver projects on time
- Reduce development costs
- Increase process maturity
- Support continuous improvement

---

## 3. CMMI Maturity Levels

CMMI defines **5 Maturity Levels**.

### Level 1 – Initial

#### Characteristics

- Processes are ad hoc and chaotic
- Success depends on individual effort
- No standard procedures

#### Example

- Developers work differently on every project
- Documentation is inconsistent

---

### Level 2 – Managed

#### Characteristics

- Basic project management practices exist
- Requirements are tracked
- Project planning is performed

#### Example

- Project schedules are created
- Risks are identified
- Progress is monitored

---

### Level 3 – Defined

#### Characteristics

- Organization-wide standard processes
- Processes are documented and standardized
- Best practices are shared

#### Example

- Every project follows the same development methodology
- Standard templates are used

---

### Level 4 – Quantitatively Managed

#### Characteristics

- Processes are measured using statistical techniques
- Performance is predictable

#### Example

- Defect rates are analyzed
- Productivity metrics are monitored
- Quality goals are measured

---

### Level 5 – Optimizing

#### Characteristics

- Continuous process improvement
- Innovation and defect prevention
- Root cause analysis

#### Example

- Continuous improvement programs
- Automation initiatives
- Lessons learned incorporated into processes

---

## 4. CMMI Maturity Level Summary

| Level | Name | Focus |
|---------|---------|---------|
| 1 | Initial | Ad hoc processes |
| 2 | Managed | Basic project management |
| 3 | Defined | Standardized organizational processes |
| 4 | Quantitatively Managed | Metrics and statistical control |
| 5 | Optimizing | Continuous improvement |

---

## 5. CMMI vs ISO 9001

| Aspect | CMMI | ISO 9001 |
|----------|----------|----------|
| Purpose | Process improvement | Quality management system |
| Focus | Software and systems processes | Organization-wide quality |
| Assessment | Maturity levels | Certification |
| Levels | 1–5 | No maturity levels |
| Goal | Process capability improvement | Compliance with standards |

---

## 6. CMMI and Software Quality Management

CMMI supports Software Quality Management through:

### Quality Assurance

- Process audits
- Compliance checks
- Process improvement

### Quality Control

- Defect tracking
- Testing
- Reviews and inspections

### Measurement

- Defect density
- Productivity
- Customer satisfaction
- Schedule performance
